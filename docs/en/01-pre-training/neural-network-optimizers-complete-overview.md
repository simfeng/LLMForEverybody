(Maybe) the most complete overview of neural network optimizers on the whole internet.

Some time ago, I wanted to understand the history of optimizer development and the path from gradient descent to today's most common choice, AdamW. But after reading a lot of material, I still could not find a complete enough overview. So I decided to put one together myself. I hope it helps.

An optimizer updates model parameters during training. Its goal is to adjust parameters to minimize the loss function, meaning the gap between the model's predictions and the real data.

From 1951, when Herbert Robbins and Sutton Monro proposed SGD in "A Stochastic Approximation Method," to 2017, when AdamW became the mainstream choice, optimizer development has already spanned more than 70 years. In this series, we look at neural network optimizers from a historical perspective and hope it helps you understand how optimizers evolved.
